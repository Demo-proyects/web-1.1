Your Marketing Works

Your Website Sells.

What would happen if every person who sees your videos could explore your entire catalog in 2 minutes, without endless scrolling or waiting for responses? You've already built a solid audience. Thousands of people see your products and are interested. But today, most don't buy because the process is too complicated.

1. Better shopping experience = more sales

When someone sees your videos on Instagram and wants to explore more, what happens? They have to scroll endlessly, search through old posts, and try to find what interests them. For many users, that's frustrating, and that's why most don't explore your complete catalog. With a website, the experience is completely different. Customers can browse by categories, use filters, see related products, and discover more options in a clear and organized way. When people spend more time exploring comfortably, they naturally end up buying more.

2. Fewer repetitive questions, more time to sell

How many times a day do you receive the same questions? "Do you have size M?", "What colors does it come in?", "How much is shipping?". A website answers all of that automatically, 24 hours a day. Customers can see available sizes, colors, prices, and important details before contacting you. When they arrive at WhatsApp, they already know what they want; all that's left is to close the sale.

But there's more: responding to a message only solves one customer's doubt. If their size or the color they're looking for isn't available, the sale is often lost right there. Chat doesn't encourage the customer to explore more.

On the other hand, with a website the customer doesn't stop to ask: they search for information on their own, browse the catalog, and discover other products. If they don't find exactly what they were looking for, they keep exploring until they find something they do want to buy.

That's why less time answering basic questions means more time for the customer exploring products and buying.

3. You're losing customers who already want to buy

You did the hardest part: you built a massive audience with hundreds of thousands of real followers and daily content that proves you're an active and trustworthy business. The problem is that all that solid foundation only works within Instagram.

Every time someone nearby searches Google for the products you sell, Google decides which business to show. It doesn't choose randomly. It looks for clear signals: verifiable physical location, brand with solid digital presence, and constant commercial activity. Most Instagram boutiques don't qualify. You have it all, but there's a problem: Google can't show an Instagram profile in search results. It needs a website.

Without that, you're completely invisible to all those potential customers who are searching to buy exactly what you offer. With a website, you appear automatically when someone searches in your area, without depending on followers or the changing algorithms of social networks. And there's more: you can pay to guarantee you appear in the top positions when someone searches for your products. It's not generic advertising to "generate interest"; it's appearing precisely when someone has already decided to buy and is searching for where to do it.

You built an extremely solid foundation, perfect for Google, and you're not building anything on top of it. You already have everything Google needs to trust your business and send you customers. All that's missing is the website to activate all that advantage.

What impact does a website have on businesses like yours? Studies and the experience of many businesses with solid marketing show that integrating a professional website usually generates:

Better conversion of existing traffic, without needing to increase followers.
Fewer basic questions, more time for customers exploring products.
Higher value per customer, as people buy more than they had planned.
More trust and credibility by perceiving the business as more serious.
Progressive growth in sales and brand recognition.
Google positioning, attracting customers who don't follow the brand on social media.

Customers who return and higher value per subscriber: when buying from you is clearly easier and more comfortable than with the competition, people don't just buy once, but subscribe and save your site to return when they want to buy again. This reduces the need to constantly be looking for new followers and, at the same time, makes it easier for your follower base to grow. As a result, each subscriber is worth much more than before, because they'll always return the moment they need to buy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you work only with clothing stores?

No. My main focus is e-commerce, mainly because where I live there are many stores with strong online visibility but no website. I don't limit myself exclusively to clothing brands, but at the moment I work alone, so I can't actively pursue many different industries at once. That said, I can work with different types of businesses. I would simply need some time to understand the market context, analyze its problems, and design the right solutions. When I handle the full process alone, content strategy is what takes the most time. If I'm hired only for development or design, scalability is not an issue.

Can I hire you for only one part of the project?

Yes, and in fact, that's ideal. By focusing on a single part of the project, I can deliver higher-quality results. I can also provide multiple options to choose from instead of a single proposal, allowing you to select the direction that fits your business best. With AI accelerating code writing, I can now deliver fast and varied results, then refine and optimize the option that makes the most sense for you.

What technologies do you actually use?

For my personal projects, I often use vanilla JavaScript. This choice is mainly for my clients' convenience—especially businesses that don't yet have a website due to limited technical knowledge—because it makes the code easier to understand, visualize, and modify. That said, I can work with the technology that best fits your needs. I'm comfortable working with React, Next.js, or WordPress (primarily as a CMS). If you specifically want your project built in WordPress or another stack, that's absolutely possible.

Will the site be easy to manage afterwards?

Yes. Whenever the project allows, I deliver systems that the client can manage without depending on the developer, with clear structures and documentation when necessary.

What is the work process like?

First I clearly define what is expected from the project and what the goals are. Then I analyze the best way to implement it and keep the client informed during the entire process, sharing progress and explaining important decisions. This avoids surprises and ensures that the final result is aligned with expectations.

How long does an average project take?

It depends on the scope. Development-only projects are fast. Full design and development take longer due to market analysis and iterative proposals. Content is usually what consumes the most time; when the client already has it defined, the project moves much faster.

Do you work alone or with a team?

I work independently, but I can collaborate with other profiles when the project requires it.

Do you offer support after delivery?

Yes. I offer post-delivery support and detailed explanations (including video) so you can manage your project independently, without depending on constant maintenance.
